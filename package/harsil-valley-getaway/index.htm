Daywise Itinerary

Day 01: Arrival in Harsil

  • Travel: Take a shared jeep or public bus from Dehradun or Rishikesh to Uttarkashi, then transfer to Harsil.
  • Accommodation: Check-in to a budget-friendly homestay in Harsil.
  • Activities: Spend the evening walking along the Bhagirathi River and capturing sunset views over the mountains.
  • Dinner: Enjoy a home-cooked local meal at your homestay.

Day 02: Trek to Sattal

  • Morning: Begin an early trek to Sattal, a group of seven interconnected lakes hidden in the dense forest. Capture reflections of the mountains in the water and enjoy a packed lunch by the lakeside.
  • Afternoon: Return to Harsil and spend the evening relaxing by the Bhagirathi River.
  • Dinner: Savor a local dish like rajma-chawal (kidney beans and rice) at your homestay.

Day 03: Visit Gangotri

  • Morning: Take a shared taxi or local bus to Gangotri, one of the holiest pilgrimage sites in India. The drive itself offers stunning views of the Himalayas.
  • Afternoon: Spend time exploring the Gangotri Temple and capturing the spiritual energy of the location. On your return, stop at Bhairon Ghati, a beautiful location for landscape photography.
  • Evening: Enjoy a quiet evening in Harsil, relaxing by a bonfire.

Day 04: Hike to Dharali Village

  • Morning: Embark on a half-day hike to Dharali Village, known for its traditional wooden houses and apple orchards. During autumn, the village is a vibrant mix of orange and red hues.
  • Afternoon: Return to Harsil and explore the deodar forests for more photography opportunities.
  • Evening: Rest at your homestay and enjoy a home-cooked meal.

Day 05: Deaprture

Have a leisurely breakfast before heading back to Uttarkashi and onward to Dehradun or Rishikesh.

Harsil Valley Getaway

Harsil Village, located at an altitude of 2,620 meters (8,600 feet), is the heart of Harsil Valley, the village is surrounded by majestic snow-capped peaks, lush deodar forests, and apple orchards. The peace and solitude you experience here are unmatched, making it an ideal spot for those seeking a break from city life.

What is Harsil Valley Famous For?

  • Harsil Valley is known for its Apple Orchards, which was introduced by a British settler Fredric E Wilson.
  • This was the place where legendary filmmaker Raj Kapoor had shot parts of his super-hit movie Ram Teri Ganga Maili. 
  • Nelong Valley is another touristy area in the neighborhood of Harsil which shares its boundaries with Tibet region. 

 

What is The Best Time to Visit Harsil Valley?

Harsil Valley welcomes visitors all year round. However, the most enjoyable times to visit are from April to June. During these months, the weather is pleasant, not too cold or hot, making it a great time for travel.

  • Winters in Harsil Valley:
    • Nights drop to 0 degrees Celsius, days at 10 degrees Celsius.
    • January and February bring heavy snow, with road closures at times.
    • Snowfall peaks in early February, melting gradually in March.
  • A Summer Retreat in Harsil Valley:
    • May and June bring the busiest time with temperatures around 20 degrees Celsius in the day and 10 degrees Celsius at night.
    • It’s when Harsil is buzzing with tourists enjoying the pleasant weather and vibrant surroundings.
