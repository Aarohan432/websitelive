Weather Conditions at Char Dham Yatra

June sees temperatures between 10°C to 20°C at Yamunotri, 8°C to 18°C at Gangotri, 5°C to 15°C at Kedarnath, and 8°C to 20°C at Badrinath. Thorough preparation is essential, and you can find detailed weather info on the official website.

Weather Conditions at Char Dham Yatra

May & June Temperatures: Yamunotri 5-15°C, Gangotri 6-16°C, Kedarnath 2-12°C, Badrinath 5-17°C.
September & October Temperatures: Yamunotri 03-20°C, Gangotri 8-18°C, Kedarnath 5-15°C, Badrinath 8-20°C.

What is the Best Time to Visit Yamunotri Temple ?

  • Summer (May to June): Pleasant temperatures ranging from 17°C to 35°C makes it the best time to visit Yamunotri Temple.
  • Monsoon (July to September): Heavy rainfall can lead to landslides, making the journey challenging.
  • Autumn (October to November): The temperature drops, and snowfall may begin, indicating the approaching winter. 12°C to 30°C

Yamunotri Weather in May

May month brings a mild climate to Yamunotri, Uttarakhand, with daytime temperatures ranging from 10°C to 20°C. Nights are cool at around 5°C. Pilgrims enjoy the pleasant weather as it is the best time to visit Yamunotri, but one should carry layers and be prepared for occasional rain.

Weather Conditions at Gangotri

  • Summer (May to June): Mild temperatures of 6°C to 18°C create a comfortable environment for pilgrims.
  • Monsoon (July to September): Like Yamunotri, Gangotri experiences heavy rainfall, and caution is advised.
  • Autumn (October to November): As winter approaches, temperatures drop, and snowfall becomes more frequent.4°C to 15°C

What is The Best Time to Visit Gangotri Temple ?

The best time to visit Gangotri Temple is from May to June and September to October when temperatures range from 6°C to 20°C. Approximately 460 km from Delhi (12-13 hours by road) and 250 km from Haridwar (8-9 hours).

Check out our blog “Gangotri Temple” for more insights.

Weather Conditions at Kedarnath

  • Summer (May to June): Daytime temperatures range from 5°C to 20°C, offering a pleasant journey.
  • Monsoon (July to September): Intermittent rainfall and the risk of landslides make this period challenging.
  • Autumn (October to November): The weather gets colder, with snow starting to cover the region. -2°C to 12°C

Which month is safe to visit Kedarnath ?

The safest time to visit Kedarnath is from May to June and September to October. During these months, temperatures range from 5°C to 20°C. It’s advisable to avoid the monsoon season (July to August) due to heavy rainfall and potential landslides.

Best Time to Visit Kedarnath For Snowfall

Best Time to Visit Kedarnath For Snowfall is from late October to early March. Plan your visit in October or November to witness enchanting snowfall, The temple is open until Diwali every year. but be prepared for chilly temperatures ranging from -5°C to 5°C.

Weather Conditions at Badrinath

  • Summer (May to June): Enjoy temperatures between 5°C to 18°C for a comfortable pilgrimage.
  • Monsoon (July to September): Rainfall is common, and caution is crucial during this period.
  • Autumn (October to November): The weather becomes colder, and snowfall increases, signaling the onset of winter. 0°C to 12°C

What is The Best Time To Visit Badrinath ?

The optimal time to visit Badrinath is from May to June and September to October when temperatures range from 7°C to 18°C. Approximately 320 km from Haridwar (10 hours by road) and 550 km from Delhi (17 hours by road).

What to Pack For Char Dham Yatra ?

  • Layered Clothing
  • Rain Gear
  • Sturdy Footwear
  • Essential Medications and First Aid
  • Hydration and Nutrition
  • Emergency Supplies
  • Communication Devices
  1. Layered Clothing:
    • Pack clothing in layers to adapt to temperature variations at different altitudes. This ensures comfort during both chilly mornings and warmer afternoons.
  2. Rain Gear:
    • Carry a reliable rain jacket, poncho, and waterproof footwear to stay dry during unexpected rainfall. Monsoons can bring sudden downpours, especially in July to September.
  3. Sturdy Footwear:
    • Opt for durable and comfortable footwear with good traction. Sturdy shoes are essential for navigating uneven terrains and ensuring a secure footing, especially in areas prone to landslides.
  4. Regular Weather Checks:
    • Stay informed about the weather conditions along the Char Dham Yatra route. Check reliable sources for real-time updates to adjust your plans and be prepared for any unforeseen changes.
  5. Essential Medications and First Aid:
    • Pack a basic first aid kit with essential medications. Altitude sickness can be a concern, so consult with a healthcare professional and carry medications prescribed for high altitudes.
  6. Hydration and Nutrition:
    • Stay hydrated and maintain a nutritious diet. High altitudes can increase the risk of dehydration, and a well-balanced diet helps in maintaining energy levels during the physically demanding journey.
  7. Emergency Supplies:
    • Pack emergency essentials such as a flashlight, extra batteries, a multipurpose tool, and a whistle. These items can be invaluable in unexpected situations and provide a sense of security during the pilgrimage.
  8. Communication Devices:
    • Carry a fully charged mobile phone with a power bank. Having a reliable means of communication is crucial in case of emergencies or if you need to stay connected with fellow pilgrims or family members.

Best Time Saving Char Dham Tour Plan from Haridwar

Creating the best Char Dham Yatra plan from Haridwar involves careful consideration of the travel route, accommodation options, and the time needed at each destination. Here’s a suggested itinerary for a well-rounded and enjoyable Char Dham Yatra in 2025.

Day 1: Haridwar to Barkot

  • Depart from Haridwar early in the morning.
  • Enroute, visit Mussoorie for a short stop to enjoy the scenic beauty.
  • Reach Barkot in the evening, check into a comfortable hotel, and rest for the night.

Day 2: Barkot to Yamunotri and back to Barkot

  • Start early for Janki Chatti, the base for the trek to Yamunotri.
  • Trek to Yamunotri Dham and visit the Yamunotri temple.
  • Return to Barkot in the evening and relax.

Day 3: Barkot to Uttarkashi

  • Depart for Uttarkashi after breakfast.
  • Visit the Kashi Vishwanath Temple and relax by the Bhagirathi River in the evening.
  • Overnight stay in Uttarkashi.

Day 4: Uttarkashi to Gangotri and back to Uttarkashi

  • Drive to Gangotri, one of the Char Dhams, and visit the Gangotri Temple.
  • Return to Uttarkashi in the evening and explore the local market.
  • Overnight stay in Uttarkashi.

Day 5: Uttarkashi to Guptkashi

  • Depart for Guptkashi, a significant stop on the Char Dham Yatra route.
  • Visit the Vishwanath Temple in Guptkashi.
  • Check into a hotel and rest for the night.

Day 6: Guptkashi to Kedarnath

  • Begin the journey to Gaurikund, the base for the Kedarnath trek.
  • Trek or take a helicopter to Kedarnath and visit the Kedarnath Temple.
  • Overnight stay in Kedarnath.

Day 7: Kedarnath to Pipalkoti

  • Descend from Kedarnath to Gaurikund and proceed towards Pipalkoti.
  • Check into a hotel and relax after the strenuous trek.
  • Overnight stay in Pipalkoti.

Day 8: Pipalkoti to Badrinath

  • Travel to Badrinath, the final destination of the Char Dham Yatra.
  • Visit the Badrinath Temple and take a dip in the Tapt Kund.
  • Explore Mana Village, the last village before the Indo-China border.
  • Overnight stay in Badrinath.

Day 9: Badrinath to Rudraprayag

  • Depart from Badrinath and drive to Rudraprayag.
  • Visit Rudraprayag’s confluence of the Alaknanda and Mandakini rivers.
  • Check into a hotel and relax for the night.

Day 10: Rudraprayag to Haridwar

  • Begin the return journey to Haridwar.
  • Enroute, visit Rishikesh for a spiritual experience and adventure activities.
  • Reach Haridwar by evening, completing the Char Dham Yatra.

This itinerary allows for a balanced and comprehensive experience of the Char Dham Yatra, taking into account the spiritual significance of each destination and ensuring adequate rest and exploration time. Adjustments can be made based on individual preferences and travel constraints.
