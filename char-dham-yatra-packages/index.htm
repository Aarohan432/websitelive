Places To Visit

Your Char Dham Yatra is packed with fascinating sites. Visit historic temples, legendary spots from the Mahabharata, and other religious landmarks. Each stop offers a glimpse into India’s rich spiritual and cultural past, making your journey both enlightening and memorable.

Lakhamandal Temple

Witnessed the birth of the world’s first Shivlinga, first worshiped by Vishnu & Brahma

Harsil Valley

Apple Orchards of Harsil produces some of the finest apples in India.

Triyuginarayan Temple

Known for its eternal fire which is burning continuously since the divine marriage of Lord Shiva and Goddess Parvati.

Chopta

Serves as the gateway to the highest Shiva temple in the world and one of the Panch Kedar pilgrimage sites.

Tungnath Temple

Highest Shiva temple in Asia, it’s also a part of Panch Kedar.

Mana Village

First Indian village on the Indo-Tibetan border, known for its cultural and scenic significance.

Devprayag Sangam

Devprayag where Alaknanda River joins Bhagirathi forming holy river Ganga, which flows down to the plains.

Dhari Devi Temple

One of the 108 Shakti sthals, the goddess protects the Char Dham of Uttarakhand.

How Much Time Does It Take to Complete Char Dham Yatra ?

Typically, completing the Char Dham Yatra takes around 10 to 12 days. However, the duration can vary based on the chosen itinerary, travel preferences, and individual pacing.

Char Dham Yatra from Haridwar – 10Days

  • It takes minimum 9-10 days to complete Char Dham yatra from Haridwar, this is the quickest one can finish this yatra if running short on time.
  • Starting from haridwar, 2N Yamunotri, 2N gangotri, 2N Kedarnath and 2N Badrinath
  • Check out the shortest itinerary for Char Dham Yatra from Haridwar

Char Dham Yatra from Delhi – 12 Days

  • You will require minimum 11 days of time to complete Char Dham Yatra from Delhi.
  • Starting from New Delhi, 1N haridwar, 2N Yamunotri, 2N gangotri, 2N Kedarnath, 2N Badrinath and 1N Rishikesh.
