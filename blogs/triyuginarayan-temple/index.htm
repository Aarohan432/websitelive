Triyuginarayan Temple – Lord Shiva’s Wedding Spot

Triyuginarayan Temple, known for its eternal fire which is burning continuously since the divine marriage of Lord Shiva and Goddess Parvati. The temple is located in the serene landscapes of Uttarakhand’s Garhwal region, stands as the sacred spot of Lord Shiva’s celestial wedding.

What is Triyuginarayan Temple famous for?

Triyuginarayan Temple, famed for hosting Lord Shiva and Parvati’s eternal marriage, attracts pilgrims. Its perpetual fire, believed to have witnessed the divine union, adds to the religious allure.

  • The temple’s attraction comes from its old Vedic style seen in the detailed stone carvings on its walls.
  • Decorated with special Yantra designs thought to hold powerful spiritual energy, inviting deep thinking.
  • Holy ponds and a forever-burning flame show purity and endless divine love, attracting believers seeking blessings.
  • It was Lord Rama’s home during his exile, making it more mysterious and special because of its connection to the epic Ramayana.

The Ultimate Destination Wedding Spot

  • The temple is especially known for hosting weddings. Legend says that couples marrying here receive blessings from Lord Vishnu, Lord Shiva, and Goddess Parvati.
  • Triyuginarayan Temple wedding ceremonies
  • create a special and sacred atmosphere, making it a beloved spot for couples seeking blessings for their union.
  • Triyuginarayan Temple wedding cost could vary depending on various factors such as decorations, rituals, accommodations, and other arrangements needed for a wedding ceremony at this sacred site in Uttarakhand, India.


Which celebrity got married in Triyuginarayan Temple?


In 2017, television actress Kavita Kaushik, known for her role as Chandramukhi Chautala in the popular TV series FIR, chose Triyuginarayan temple for her wedding. Besides, the marriage of IPS officer Aparna Gautam from Ghaziabad and ADM Lalit Mohan from Nainital also happened at this revered temple. The state government’s decision to promote it as a wedding venue has drawn considerable attention, making it a favored choice for weddings, even among international couples.

A Perfect Spot for Pilgrims

  • The temple is a key stop for Char Dham Yatra pilgrims, drawing seekers of spiritual growth and inner peace.
  • Rituals performed here provide salvation to ancestors, releasing them from the cycle of rebirth.
  • Colorful festivals bring a sense of spiritual vitality, celebrating the temple’s cultural heritage.

How far is Triyuginarayan from kedarnath?

Triyuginarayan Temple is 15 km from Gaurikund, the Kedarnath trek starting point. Kedarnath is an additional 16 km from Gaurikund. Allocate 3-4 hours for a visit during Char Dham Yatra.

Do we have to trek for Triyuginarayan Temple?

No trekking is needed for Triyuginarayan Temple; a separate 12-kilometer road from Sonprayag is accessible by commercial and private vehicles. During peak season, consider extra time and opt for smaller vehicles to navigate narrower roads, avoiding congestion.

Which is the nearest railway station to Triyuginarayan Temple?

Yog-Nagari Rishikesh Railway Station is the nearest railway station to Triyuginarayan Temple, approximately 214 kilometers away. From Rishikesh, one can hire taxis or use public transportation to reach the temple.

Triyuginarayan & Kedarnath Trek Itinerary

Day 1: Arrival and Acclimatization

  • Arrive at the base town of Gaurikund.
  • Acclimatize to the altitude and weather conditions.
  • Rest and prepare for the trek.

Day 2: Gaurikund to Kedarnath (17 Kms Trek)

  • Start trekking from Gaurikund early in the morning.
  • Trek through lush green landscapes and alongside the Mandakini River.
  • Pass through Rambara and reach Kedarnath.
  • Visit the Kedarnath Temple upon arrival.
  • Overnight stay in Kedarnath.

Day 3: Kedarnath to Gaurikund

  • Descend back to Gaurikund.
  • Enjoy the scenic beauty along the way.
  • Reach Gaurikund by afternoon.
  • proceed to Triyuginarayan, return hotel by evening.
  • Rest and relax.

Day 4: Reserve Day or Additional Sightseeing

  • Keep a reserve day in case of unforeseen circumstances or bad weather.
  • Optionally, explore nearby areas or visit local attractions.

Note: The itinerary can be adjusted based on your pace, preferences, and the prevailing weather conditions. It’s crucial to stay updated on the weather forecast and any potential changes to the trekking route. Always follow safety guidelines and consult with local authorities or trek organizers.

Best Time to Visit Triyuginarayan Temple

Triyuginarayan temple welcomes visitors all year round. However, the most enjoyable times to visit are from September to November and from April to June. During these months, the weather is pleasant, not too cold or hot, making it a great time for travel.

How to Reach Triyuginarayan Temple

  1. By Road: Triyuginarayan Temple to Kedarnath distance is 13 Kilometers and it is easily accessible via the national highway which connects to Dehradun, Uttarakhand’s capital.
  2. By Air: Jolly Grant Airport Dehradun, approximately 226 kilometers from the temple.
  3. By Railway: Rishikesh railway station, around 212 kilometers away, serves as the nearest railway station.

FAQs

  1. How to reach Triyuginarayan Temple and what are the stay option?
    • Triyuginarayan Temple can be reached by road from Rishikesh and Haridwar. Accommodations near the temple cater to various budgets.
  2. Is there any entry fee in Triyuginarayan Temple?
    • No entry fee; the temple welcomes all, irrespective of religious beliefs. Modest attire is recommended.
  3. What is the best time to visit Triyuginarayan Temple?
    • Ideal months for visiting are April-June and September-November.
  4. Is there any place to see around Triyuginarayan Temple?
    • Nearby attractions include Kedarnath and Chopta Valley.
  5. Can we perform wedding at Triyuginarayan Temple?
    • The temple hosts Hindu wedding ceremonies, enveloping couples in divine blessings.
  6. Is there any trekking route to reach Triyuginarayan Temple?
    • No, there is very good conectivity of roads from national highways.
