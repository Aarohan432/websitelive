Haridwar
Haridwar is one of the holiest cities in India and a popular starting point for spiritual journeys in Uttarakhand. The city sits on the banks of the Ganges River and is best known for the evening Ganga Aarti at Har Ki Pauri.

Rishikesh
Rishikesh is a peaceful town known for yoga, meditation, and river adventure. Located just 20 km from Haridwar, it is often called the Yoga Capital of the World.

Kedarnath
Kedarnath is one of the most important Shiva temples in India, located high in the Himalayas at over 11,000 feet.

Badrinath
Badrinath is a peaceful temple town located between the Nar and Narayan mountains, dedicated to Lord Vishnu.

Nainital
Nainital is a beautiful lake town set in a valley, with Naini Lake at its center. Visitors can enjoy boating and walk around Mall Road.

Jim Corbett National Park
Jim Corbett National Park is India's first tiger reserve and a great spot for wildlife lovers. Visitors come here for jeep safaris and birdwatching.

Spiritual Experiences in Uttarakhand
Uttarakhand is known as Dev Bhoomi, the land of gods. Thousands of pilgrims visit every year to complete the Char Dham Yatra — Kedarnath, Badrinath, Gangotri, and Yamunotri.

Leisure Holidays in Uttarakhand
If your idea of a holiday means fresh air, mountain views, and doing absolutely nothing — Uttarakhand has you covered. Popular hill stations like Nainital, Mussoorie, Bhimtal, and Lansdowne are perfect for relaxed getaways.

Adventure Activities in Uttarakhand
From white-water rafting in Rishikesh to snow skiing in Auli, there's plenty to do. Try trekking in Chopta, Tungnath, or Valley of Flowers.

Road Trip Routes in Uttarakhand
There's nothing like a road trip through the hills of Uttarakhand. The drives from Rishikesh to Auli or Delhi to Nainital are full of turns, views, and roadside chai spots.

Wildlife Safaris in Uttarakhand
Jim Corbett National Park is India's oldest and one of the most exciting wildlife reserves. Go on a safari to spot tigers, elephants, deer, and rare birds.

Family-Friendly Places in Uttarakhand
Planning a trip with kids or elders? Uttarakhand has many easy and comfortable options. Nainital, Mussoorie, Bhimtal, and Jim Corbett are all well-connected.

Romantic Getaways in Uttarakhand
Couples looking for peace, views, and quiet time together will love Uttarakhand. Auli, Mukteshwar, Kanatal, and Bhimtal are some of the top romantic destinations.

Homestay Experiences in Uttarakhand
If you want to skip touristy hotels and feel the real vibe of the hills, stay in a homestay. Uttarakhand has many local-run stays in villages near Almora, Tehri, Mukteshwar, and Chopta.

Top Spiritual Tours In Uttarakhand

Uttarakhand is full of places that people visit for faith, peace, and quiet. From the evening aarti in Haridwar to the long walk to Kedarnath, every spot has its own feeling. Some come for blessings, some for the journey, and some just to sit by the river or visit an old temple. If you’re looking for meaningful places in the hills, these are worth adding to your list.

Read MoreLess

Haridwar
One of the oldest and most visited holy cities in India, Haridwar is where the Ganga enters the plains. The Ganga Aarti at Har Ki Pauri is the highlight, drawing pilgrims and travelers every evening. It’s also a starting point for the Char Dham Yatra. With its temples, ghats, and constant hum of prayers, Haridwar offers a strong spiritual presence without needing much effort to find it.

Kedarnath
Kedarnath is one of the most important Shiva temples in India, and part of the Char Dham circuit. Reaching it takes effort—either a long uphill trek or a helicopter ride—but the setting is unmatched. Surrounded by mountains and glaciers, the temple stands strong and simple. It’s a place that many visit once, and remember forever.

Badrinath
Dedicated to Lord Vishnu, Badrinath is located along the Alaknanda River and is also part of the Char Dham. The temple is colourful and welcoming, and the surrounding mountains make it feel peaceful even when it’s crowded. Many people combine it with a visit to Mana, the last village before the Indo-China border. It’s easy to reach by road, and the drive itself is part of the experience.

Kainchi Dham
Near Nainital, Kainchi Dham is a peaceful ashram dedicated to Neem Karoli Baba. It’s not as big or busy as other places, but it’s known for its quiet power. Many followers of Baba—including well-known tech leaders—have visited for peace and clarity. The ashram is simple, surrounded by trees and hills, and leaves a calm feeling even if you stay for just a short while.

Char Dham
The full Char Dham Yatra includes Yamunotri, Gangotri, Kedarnath, and Badrinath. It’s one of the most important pilgrimages in Hindu tradition. The journey takes you deep into the Himalayas, across rivers, forests, and mountain roads. Some people complete it in one trip, others do it over time. Each stop has its own meaning, and the entire route is as much about the road as it is about the temples.

Hemkund Sahib
Hemkund Sahib is a Sikh pilgrimage site set at high altitude near the Valley of Flowers. The trek is steep and the weather unpredictable, but the destination—a gurudwara beside a glacial lake—is worth it. It’s peaceful, quiet, and full of respect. Many visit during the summer months when the snow melts and the path is open. It’s a journey people take not just for religion, but for personal connection.

Luxury Escapes In Uttarakhand

Uttarakhand isn’t just for yatras and treks, some places here are made purely for slowing down and staying in comfort. If you’re someone who enjoys mountain views, quiet mornings, and a bit of pampering, these luxury resorts are totally worth looking into. They’re not budget-friendly, but they deliver value, especially if you’re celebrating something or just want to unwind in comfort.

Taj Rishikesh Resort & Spa

Singthali, near Shivpuri
This luxury resort is located about 30 km from Rishikesh town, away from the crowd, right next to the Ganga river. Rooms have forest or river views, and it’s a great pick for wellness getaways—there’s a full spa, yoga pavilion, and riverside deck.

Best for: Couples, wellness travelers, peace seekers
Approx Price: ₹20,000–₹35,000/night
Good to Know: Limited phone network; ideal for digital detox

The Naini Retreat, Nainital

Ayarpata Slopes, Nainital
Originally a royal residence, now a heritage-style hotel with modern upgrades. Just a 10-minute drive from Mall Road but feels private and calm. Rooms have wooden interiors, and there’s a great view of Naini Lake from the lawns.

Best for: Couples, small families, heritage lovers
Approx Price: ₹10,000–₹18,000/night
Good to Know: Gets sold out on weekends and holidays; book early

JW Marriott Mussoorie Walnut Grove Resort & Spa

Barlowganj, Mussoorie
One of Mussoorie’s most premium stays, about 5 km from Mall Road. The resort offers big valley views, indoor pool, multiple restaurants, and lots of space for kids. Rooms are modern with all comforts and large windows.

Best for: Families, honeymooners, special occasions
Approx Price: ₹18,000–₹30,000/night
Good to Know: Great for longer stays (2–3 nights); check for meal packages

Namah Resort, Jim Corbett

Dhikuli, near Bijrani Zone
Located just 8 km from Ramnagar, this is a comfortable, full-service resort near Corbett National Park. It has lawns, a pool, spa, and large rooms—some with river views. Ideal if you want luxury near safari zones.

Best for: Wildlife lovers, seniors, relaxed holidays
Approx Price: ₹9,000–₹16,000/night
Good to Know: Safaris need to be pre-booked; resort can help with that

Top Weekend Getaways In Uttarakhand

If you’re short on time but still want fresh air, quiet views, or a bit of adventure—these Uttarakhand spots are perfect for 2–3 day trips. All six places are well-connected by road, have good stays, and offer something different depending on what kind of weekend you’re looking for.

Read MoreLess

Jim Corbett is one of the closest and most exciting options. It’s around 6 to 7 hours from Delhi and ideal for families or nature lovers. Morning jeep safaris in zones like Bijrani or Dhikala offer chances to see tigers, elephants, and deer. The area around Dhikuli has nice riverside resorts and forest views. If you’re going on a weekend, make sure to book your safari permits at least 10–15 days in advance.

Auli is a bit farther but perfect for snow lovers and peaceful views. It’s best visited between December and February for snow, but even in summer it’s quiet and scenic. You’ll reach Joshimath first, and then take the famous cable car up to Auli. The views from the ropeway and the mountain stay options are totally worth the effort. If there’s heavy snowfall, the road might close, so parking in Joshimath and riding the cable car is the safest bet.

Rishikesh is always a solid weekend plan. Only 5–6 hours from Delhi, you get adventure, riverside stays, and a relaxed vibe all in one. Rafting from Shivpuri is popular between October and June, while yoga retreats and Ganga aarti at Triveni Ghat draw spiritual travelers year-round. Tapovan is the best area to stay if you want good cafés, rafting access, and a bit of peace away from the main town.

Nainital is great if you want something easy and scenic. It’s around 7 hours away and works well for couples or families. You can walk along Mall Road, take a boat ride on Naini Lake, or go up to Snow View Point. If you stay slightly away from the lake—say, Ayarpata Slopes—you’ll get better views and quieter surroundings, but still be close enough to explore town on foot.

Mussoorie is another quick hill trip, just over 6 hours via Dehradun. It’s perfect for mountain views, colonial cafés, and short walks like Camel’s Back Road or Lal Tibba. Families love the central locations, but Landour offers a more peaceful, boutique vibe. Try to avoid peak tourist weekends in May and June—it gets crowded fast.

Chopta is for travelers who like the mountains raw and real. It takes around 10–12 hours to reach, but once you’re there, it’s totally worth it. You’ll find pine forests, snow in winter, and green meadows in summer. It’s also the base for the Tungnath and Chandrashila trek, which is manageable even for beginners. Accommodation is limited and basic, so book early—especially if you want a warm room and a good view.
