Chopta Valley and Chandrashila Peak

Chopta Valley invites you to an eco-friendly retreat in the heart of the Himalayas, where a fully solar-powered village awaits. Serving as the gateway to the highest Shiva temple in the world, Tungnath, Chopta is also one of the Panch Kedar pilgrimage sites. This serene destination offers both spiritual and nature-loving travelers an unforgettable experience.

Where is Chopta Located?

Chopta is located in the Rudraprayag district of Uttarakhand, India. It’s a part of the Kedarnath Wildlife Sanctuary at an altitude of approximately 2,680 meters (8,790 feet) above sea level.

  • Nearest Airport: Jolly Grant Airport, Dehradun (175 kms from Chopta)
  • Nearest Railway Station: Rishikesh Railway Station (160 kms from Chopta)
  • Haridwar to Chopta Distance: 228 Kilometers, 7 to 8 Hours drive
  • Delhi to Chopta Distance: 464 Kilometers , 11 to 12 hours drive

What is Chopta Famous For?

Chopta is most famous for Tungnath-Chandrashilla Trek and the famous Deoriatal Lake Trek. Also sits in a strategic spot close to the Panch Kedar – the five holiest Shiva temples in Uttarakhand. Visualize Kedarnath and Madmaheshwar on its left, Rudranath and Kalpeshwar on its right, and right above the Tunganath temple which completes the spiritual circle.

Is Chopta Worth Visiting?

Absolutely Yes! Chopta as a destination is worth visiting for several compelling reasons. If you love trekking, it serves as a gateway to various trails, including the trek to the highest Shiva temple, Tungnath.

  • Serenity: It’s like a green paradise with beautiful mountain views.
  • Trekking Paradise: It’s perfect place for trekking lovers, Ideal for both beginners and experienced trekkers.
  • Photographer’s Delight: If you love taking pictures, the views are stunning, especially during sunrise and sunset.
  • Spiritual Connection: The spiritual aura around Tungnath Temple adds a unique charm.

Top 5 Places to see in Chopta Valley

  • Tungnath Temple
  • Chandrashila Peak
  • Deoria Taal
  • Ukhimath Omkareshwar Temple
  • Madhyamaheshwar Temple

Tungnath Temple

Tungnath Temple is the highest Shiva temple, sitting at 3,680 meters in the Himalayas. The trek from Chopta to Tungnath is about 3.5 kilometers. A sacred Panch Kedar site, it’s also a part of the ancient Kedarnath wildlife sanctuary.

Chandrashila Peak

Chandrashila Peak, at 4,000 meters in the Garhwal Himalayas, is famous for its panoramic views of Nanda Devi, Trishul, and Chaukhamba peaks. Accessible via a trek from Tungnath, it’s a popular destination for nature enthusiasts. The summit holds significance in Hindu mythology, linked to Lord Rama’s meditation.

Deoria Taal

Deoria Tal, a pristine lake in Uttarakhand, sits at 2,438 meters surrounded by dense forests. Accessible through a trek from Sari Village spanning around 2.5 kilometers, it offers reflections of Chaukhamba peaks. A serene destination, perfect for a short yet rewarding Himalayan trekking experience.

Ukhimath Omkareshwar Temple

Ukhimath, in Uttarakhand, is an important pilgrimage town. It serves as the winter home for Kedarnath idol. The old Omkareshwar Temple is a big attraction, dedicated to Lord Shiva. Visitors feel a spiritual atmosphere and cultural richness in this town, located in the Garhwal Himalayas.

Madhyamaheshwar Temple

Madhyamaheshwar Temple, located in Uttarakhand’s Garhwal Himalayas, is a sacred site dedicated to Lord Shiva, forming part of the Panch Kedar. At 3,497 meters, it offers stunning views. Accessible via a trek from Ukhimath, covering approximately 21 kilometers.

Can we go Chopta by car from Delhi?

Yes, Chopta valley is approximately 450 kms away from Delhi the 12 hours journey involves driving through scenic routes from places like Haridwar, Rihsikesh, Devprayag and Rudraprayag. Just ensure your vehicle is suitable for mountainous terrain, especially if you’re traveling during winter when there might be snow on the roads.

Is it Safe to Drive to Chopta?

Yes, it is generally safe to drive to Chopta. The distance from Delhi to Chopta is approximately 400 km, and the roads are mostly in good condition, particularly the stretch from Delhi to Rudraprayag. However, the final 30-40 km, which leads into the mountains, features narrow and winding roads typical of Himalayan terrain.

Best Time to Drive to Chopta Valley

  • Summer & Autumn (April to June, September to November): Roads are accessible, with clear weather and stable conditions.
  • Monsoon (July to September): Roads can be risky due to heavy rainfall and potential landslides.
  • Winter (December to February): Snowfall can make roads slippery, with temperatures dropping as low as -15°C.

Safety Tips Before Visiting:

  • Check weather and road conditions before your trip.
  • Avoid driving at night, especially in hilly areas.
  • If possible, hire a local driver or use a 4WD vehicle during challenging weather conditions.

How to Reach Chopta Valley from Delhi?

Chopta is around 460 Kilometers / 11-12 hours from Delhi. There is no direct bus and one may need to travel to Rishikesh, Srinagar or another popular city within Uttarakhand to reach Chopta. You can also hire a private cab from Delhi to Chopta. Chopta Tour Plan From new Delhi

Which Month is Best for Chopta?

The best time to visit Chopta is during the months from March to May when the weather is pleasant. However, It is accessible year-round, and each season brings its own unique charm.

  • Summer (March to June): Pleasant weather, ideal for trekking and sightseeing.
  • Monsoon (July to August): Lush greenery, but trekking may be challenging due to heavy rainfall.
  • Autumn (September to November): Clear skies, perfect for trekking and photography.
  • Winter (December to March): Snow-covered landscapes, especially ideal for snow enthusiasts.

Why Chopta is Called Mini Switzerland?

Chopta is also called the “Mini Switzerland of India”, offers delightful weather in summer, refreshing rainfall during monsoon, and transforms into a magical snowy wonderland in winter.

  • Serving as the focal point for Uttarakhand’s five holiest Shiva temples known as the Panch Kedar, it holds immense spiritual significance.
  • Also, It is a significant bird-watching location acknowledged by various national and international conservation and biodiversity organizations.

When Does it Snow in Chopta?

Snowfall in Chopta usually occurs during the winter months, from December to March, transforming the region into a snowy landscape. For those desiring a snowy experience, planning a visit during these winter months is recommended.

Which is Better: Auli or Chopta?

Both Auli and Chopta are stunning destinations, but have distinct characteristics. So the choice is completely depends on your preferences.

  1. Location:
    • Auli: Located at a height of approximately 3,050 meters (10,000 feet) above sea level in Chamoli district of Uttarakhand.
    • Chopta: At an altitude of 2,680 meters (8,790 feet) above sea level, It is located in the Rudraprayag district of Uttarakhand.
  2. Terrain:
    • Auli: Known for its snowy landscapes, especially appealing to winter sports enthusiasts.
    • Chopta: Characterized by lush meadows, trekking, and as a gateway to the Tungnath Temple.
  3. Activities:
    • Auli: Famous for skiing, with well-developed ski resorts and cable car rides.
    • Chopta: Ideal for trekking, nature walks, and visiting the highest Shiva temple, Tungnath.
  4. Accessibility:
    • Auli: Accessible by road or cable car from Joshimath.
    • Chopta: Accessible by road, involving a trek to reach Tungnath.

How Do You Plan a Chopta Trip?

  • Choose the Right Time: Select the season that suits your preferences.
  • Plan the Itinerary: Include Tungnath Temple, Chandrashila Summit, and Deoria Taal trek in your plan.
  • Accommodation: Book accommodations in advance, to avoid any kind of unavailability at peak seasons.
  • Transportation: Plan whether by bus, car, or a combination of both.

How many days are sufficient to visit Chopta?

Generally, a trip of 3 to 4 days is sufficient to explore the beauty of Chopta and its nearby attractions. Here’s a suggested itinerary:

  1. Day 1: Arrival
    • Arrive in Chopta, settle into your accommodation.
    • Take a leisurely stroll to soak in the surroundings.
  2. Day 2: Tungnath Temple Trek
    • Start early for the trek to Tungnath Temple.
    • Visit Chandrashila Summit if you’re up for a longer trek.
    • Return to camps in the evening.
  3. Day 3: Chopta Valley and Exploration
    • Explore Chopta Valley and its scenic beauty.
    • Visit nearby attractions like Deoria Tal if time permits.
  4. Day 4: Departure
    • Depart from Chopta, capturing any last-minute views.

Is there network problem in Chopta?

Chopta may have limited network connectivity, and you might experience weak signals, especially in certain areas or adverse weather conditions. Networks like BSNL and Jio are reported to have better connectivity in the region compared to other providers.

Is electricity available in Chopta?

There is no electricity in Chopta, but almost all guest houses have solar powered lights and even phones can be charged with the same. Carrying a power bank is recommended for uninterrupted power supply during your stay.

Is Chopta and Tungnath Same?

Chopta and Tungnath are related but distinct places. Chopta is a picturesque region known for its meadows and landscapes, while Tungnath is a sacred temple dedicated to Lord Shiva, located near Chopta.

Where is Chopta located?

Chopta is a picturesque hill station located in Uttarakhand’s Rudraprayag district, within the Kedarnath Wildlife Sanctuary. Known as the gateway to Tungnath and Chandrashila, Chopta Valley sits at an altitude of 2,680 meters, surrounded by dense forests and snow-capped peaks.

What is the best time to visit Chopta?

The best time to visit Chopta is from April to June and September to November. During these months, the weather is pleasant for trekking and outdoor activities, while the post-monsoon period offers clear skies and stunning views of the surrounding peaks.

How can I reach Chopta from Delhi?

Chopta is approximately 400 km from Delhi. You can reach by road via Haridwar and Rudraprayag, either by self-drive or by hiring a taxi. Public buses and shared taxis are also available from Haridwar and Rishikesh to Chopta Valley.

Is Chopta safe for solo travelers?

Yes, Chopta is safe for solo travelers. The area is popular among trekkers and nature lovers, with frequent tourists and local guides available. However, solo travelers should always check weather conditions, especially during winter or monsoon seasons.

What are the popular trekking routes in Chopta?

The most popular trekking route in Chopta Valley is the trek to Tungnath Temple and Chandrashila Peak. Other treks include Deoria Tal and Rohini Bugyal. These treks are moderate in difficulty, offering stunning views of the Himalayas.

How far is Tungnath from Chopta?

Tungnath Temple is about 3.5 km from Chopta and can be reached via a well-marked trekking route. The trek takes around 2-3 hours, depending on fitness level, and leads further to Chandrashila Peak, about 1.5 km from Tungnath.

Is Chopta accessible by road throughout the year?

Chopta is accessible by road most of the year. However, during heavy snowfall in winter, the roads may be blocked temporarily. It’s recommended to check road conditions in winter months before planning your trip to Chopta Valley.

What is the weather like in Chopta during winter?

Winter in Chopta is cold, with temperatures ranging from -5°C to -15°C. Snowfall is common, transforming the valley into a winter wonderland. Heavy snow may cause temporary road closures, so it’s essential to check forecasts before traveling.

Can I visit Chopta during the monsoon season?

While Chopta remains accessible during monsoon (July to September), it’s not recommended due to landslides, slippery roads, and heavy rainfall. Trekking becomes difficult and risky during this time, making summer and post-monsoon better options for a visit.

What accommodation options are available in Chopta?

Chopta offers a range of accommodation, including basic guesthouses, eco-lodges, and campsites. There are budget-friendly options available, and some packages may include tents or cottages for an immersive experience in Chopta Valley’s natural beauty.

Is there snow in Chopta during winter?

Yes, Chopta receives significant snowfall during winter, typically from December to February. The snow-covered landscapes offer breathtaking views, making it a popular destination for winter trekking to Tungnath and Chandrashila.

What are the main attractions in Chopta?

The main attractions in Chopta include Tungnath Temple, Chandrashila Peak, Deoria Tal, and the surrounding meadows of Chopta Valley. The region offers trekking, bird watching, and stunning Himalayan views, making it a paradise for nature lovers.

How difficult is the trek to Chandrashila from Chopta?

The trek to Chandrashila from Chopta is moderately difficult. The first part to Tungnath is a 3.5 km uphill trek, followed by a steeper 1.5 km hike to Chandrashila. It’s manageable for beginners with basic fitness levels but can be challenging in winter.

Are there guided tours available for Chopta?

Yes, there are several guided tour packages available for Chopta, especially for treks to Tungnath and Chandrashila. These tours typically include transportation, meals, accommodation, and experienced guides, making your Chopta Valley trip hassle-free.

How many days are required to explore Chopta?

A 3-day itinerary is ideal for exploring Chopta. Day 1 for arrival and relaxation, Day 2 for the Tungnath-Chandrashila trek, and Day 3 for a visit to Deoria Tal or a leisurely return journey. Longer trips can include more treks or nearby areas.

What should I pack for a trip to Chopta?

For a trip to Chopta, pack comfortable trekking shoes, warm clothing (especially in winter), a raincoat during monsoon, sunscreen, and basic first-aid supplies. A good backpack and trekking poles are also recommended for the Tungnath-Chandrashila trek.

Is camping allowed in Chopta?

Yes, camping is allowed in Chopta, and there are designated campsites available. Several Chopta tour packages also offer camping experiences with tents and meals provided. Camping amidst Chopta Valley’s scenic meadows is a popular activity for visitors.

Can I visit Chopta with family and kids?

Yes, Chopta is family-friendly, and many visitors come with children. The Tungnath trek may be challenging for younger kids, but the valley’s natural beauty, camping, and shorter hikes like Deoria Tal are great family activities.

What is the distance between Chopta and Haridwar?

The distance between Chopta and Haridwar is around 200 km, and the journey takes about 6-7 hours by road. The route passes through scenic locations like Rudraprayag and offers breathtaking views of the mountains.

Is Chopta a good destination for photography and bird watching?

Absolutely! Chopta Valley is a paradise for photographers and birdwatchers. The region is home to a variety of Himalayan birds, and the stunning landscapes, especially at sunrise and sunset from Chandrashila, offer incredible photo opportunities.
