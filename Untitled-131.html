Top Places In Uttarakhand

Uttarakhand is one of the top travel states in North India. It's home to famous temples like Kedarnath and Badrinath, peaceful towns like Rishikesh and Gangotri, and scenic hill stations like Mussoorie and Auli. From spiritual yatras to nature holidays, there are many places to visit in Uttarakhand that offer a good mix of faith, beauty, and local experiences.

Haridwar
Haridwar is one of the holiest cities in India and a popular starting point for spiritual journeys. The city sits on the banks of the Ganges River and is best known for the evening Ganga Aarti at Har Ki Pauri.

Rishikesh
Rishikesh is a peaceful town known for yoga, meditation, and river adventure. Located just 20 km from Haridwar, it is often called the Yoga Capital of the World.

Char Dham Yatra
The Char Dham Yatra is a sacred journey to four important temples: Yamunotri, Gangotri, Kedarnath, and Badrinath. It is one of the most meaningful spiritual experiences in India.

Yamunotri
Yamunotri is the starting point of the Char Dham Yatra and the origin of the Yamuna River. It involves a short trek from Janki Chatti, and offers hot water springs and beautiful mountain views.

Gangotri
Gangotri is a quiet temple town located on the banks of the Bhagirathi River. It marks the origin of the holy Ganga and is a key stop on the Char Dham route.

Kedarnath
Kedarnath is one of the most important Shiva temples in India, located high in the Himalayas at over 11,000 feet. The 16 km trek from Gaurikund is both challenging and rewarding.

Badrinath
Badrinath is a peaceful temple town located between the Nar and Narayan mountains. It is dedicated to Lord Vishnu and is the last stop in the Char Dham Yatra.

Mussoorie
Mussoorie is one of the most famous hill stations in Uttarakhand, known for its British-era charm and beautiful views of the Doon Valley.

Nainital
Nainital is a beautiful lake town set in a valley, with Naini Lake at its center. It's well-connected by road and rail, making it a popular weekend getaway.

Auli
Auli is a snow-covered mountain town known for skiing and panoramic Himalayan views. The ropeway from Joshimath to Auli is one of the longest in Asia.

Chopta
Chopta is a quiet place surrounded by forests and meadows, often called the Mini Switzerland of Uttarakhand. It is the base for the famous Tungnath Temple trek.

Jim Corbett National Park
Jim Corbett National Park is India's first tiger reserve and a great spot for wildlife lovers. Visitors come here for jeep safaris, birdwatching, and forest stays.

Kausani
Kausani is a peaceful hill town with wide views of Himalayan peaks like Nanda Devi and Trishul. It's also home to the Anasakti Ashram, where Mahatma Gandhi once stayed.

Your Perfect Uttarakhand Experiences

Uttarakhand isn't just one kind of trip. It's many. Some people come here for darshan at Kedarnath or Badrinath, some want to camp under the stars in Chopta, while others just want to sip tea in Mussoorie with the clouds rolling by. No matter what kind of traveller you are, there's a part of Uttarakhand that feels just right for you.

Spiritual Experiences in Uttarakhand
Uttarakhand is known as Dev Bhoomi, the land of gods. Thousands of pilgrims visit every year to complete the Char Dham Yatra — Kedarnath, Badrinath, Gangotri, and Yamunotri. Whether you seek blessings or a quiet spiritual retreat in the hills, Uttarakhand has something deeply soulful waiting for you.

Leisure Holidays in Uttarakhand
If your idea of a holiday means fresh air, mountain views, and doing absolutely nothing — Uttarakhand has you covered. Popular hill stations like Nainital, Mussoorie, Bhimtal, and Lansdowne are perfect for relaxed getaways.

Adventure Activities in Uttarakhand
From white-water rafting in Rishikesh to snow skiing in Auli, there's plenty to do. Try trekking in Chopta, Tungnath, or Valley of Flowers, or go rock climbing, ziplining, or even bungee jumping.

Road Trip Routes in Uttarakhand
There's nothing like a road trip through the hills of Uttarakhand. The drives from Rishikesh to Auli or Delhi to Nainital are full of turns, views, and roadside chai spots.

Wildlife Safaris in Uttarakhand
Jim Corbett National Park is India's oldest and one of the most exciting wildlife reserves. Go on a safari to spot tigers, elephants, deer, and rare birds.

Family-Friendly Places in Uttarakhand
Planning a trip with kids or elders? Nainital, Mussoorie, Bhimtal, and Jim Corbett are all well-connected and offer good hotels and activities for children.

Romantic Getaways in Uttarakhand
Couples looking for peace, views, and quiet time together will love Uttarakhand. Auli, Mukteshwar, Kanatal, and Bhimtal are some of the top romantic destinations.

Homestay Experiences in Uttarakhand
Uttarakhand has many local-run stays in villages near Almora, Tehri, Mukteshwar, and Chopta. You'll eat local food, learn mountain stories, and see life beyond the usual spots.

Top Spiritual Tours In Uttarakhand

Uttarakhand is full of places that people visit for faith, peace, and quiet. From the evening aarti in Haridwar to the long walk to Kedarnath, every spot has its own feeling.

Haridwar

One of the oldest and most visited holy cities in India, Haridwar is where the Ganga enters the plains. The Ganga Aarti at Har Ki Pauri is the highlight, drawing pilgrims and travelers every evening.

Kedarnath

Kedarnath is one of the most important Shiva temples in India, and part of the Char Dham circuit. Surrounded by mountains and glaciers, the temple stands strong and simple.

Badrinath

Dedicated to Lord Vishnu, Badrinath is located along the Alaknanda River. The temple is colourful and welcoming, and the surrounding mountains make it feel peaceful even when it's crowded.

Kainchi Dham

Near Nainital, Kainchi Dham is a peaceful ashram dedicated to Neem Karoli Baba. Known for its quiet power, it leaves a calm feeling even if you stay for just a short while.

Char Dham

The full Char Dham Yatra includes Yamunotri, Gangotri, Kedarnath, and Badrinath. It's one of the most important pilgrimages in Hindu tradition.

Hemkund Sahib

Hemkund Sahib is a Sikh pilgrimage site set at high altitude near the Valley of Flowers. The destination — a gurudwara beside a glacial lake — is worth the steep trek.

Luxury Escapes In Uttarakhand

Uttarakhand isn't just for yatras and treks — some places here are made purely for slowing down and staying in comfort. These luxury resorts deliver real value, especially if you're celebrating something or just want to unwind.

Taj Rishikesh Resort & Spa

Singthali, near Shivpuri

Located about 30 km from Rishikesh town, away from the crowd, right next to the Ganga river. Rooms have forest or river views — great pick for wellness getaways with a full spa, yoga pavilion, and riverside deck.

Best for: Couples, wellness travelers, peace seekers Approx Price: ₹20,000–₹35,000/night Good to Know: Limited phone network; ideal for digital detox

The Naini Retreat, Nainital

Ayarpata Slopes, Nainital

Originally a royal residence, now a heritage-style hotel with modern upgrades. Just a 10-minute drive from Mall Road but feels private and calm. Great view of Naini Lake from the lawns.

Best for: Couples, small families, heritage lovers Approx Price: ₹10,000–₹18,000/night Good to Know: Gets sold out on weekends; book early

JW Marriott Mussoorie Walnut Grove Resort & Spa

Barlowganj, Mussoorie

One of Mussoorie's most premium stays, about 5 km from Mall Road. Offers big valley views, indoor pool, multiple restaurants, and lots of space for kids.

Best for: Families, honeymooners, special occasions Approx Price: ₹18,000–₹30,000/night Good to Know: Great for longer stays (2–3 nights); check for meal packages

Namah Resort, Jim Corbett

Dhikuli, near Bijrani Zone

Located just 8 km from Ramnagar, this is a comfortable, full-service resort near Corbett National Park. Has lawns, a pool, spa, and large rooms — some with river views.

Best for: Wildlife lovers, seniors, relaxed holidays Approx Price: ₹9,000–₹16,000/night Good to Know: Safaris need to be pre-booked; resort can help with that

Top Weekend Getaways In Uttarakhand

If you're short on time but still want fresh air, quiet views, or a bit of adventure — these Uttarakhand spots are perfect for 2–3 day trips.

  • Jim CorbettOne of the closest and most exciting options — around 6–7 hours from Delhi. Morning jeep safaris in Bijrani or Dhikala offer chances to see tigers, elephants, and deer. Book safari permits at least 10–15 days in advance.
  • AuliBest visited between December and February for snow, but even in summer it's quiet and scenic. Take the famous cable car from Joshimath. Mountain stay options are totally worth the effort.
  • RishikeshOnly 5–6 hours from Delhi — adventure, riverside stays, and a relaxed vibe all in one. Rafting from Shivpuri is popular between October and June. Tapovan is the best area to stay.
  • NainitalAround 7 hours away and works well for couples or families. Walk along Mall Road, take a boat ride on Naini Lake, or go up to Snow View Point. Stay at Ayarpata Slopes for quieter surroundings.
  • MussoorieJust over 6 hours via Dehradun. Perfect for mountain views, colonial cafés, and short walks like Camel's Back Road or Lal Tibba. Landour offers a more peaceful, boutique vibe.
  • ChoptaTakes around 10–12 hours but once you're there, it's totally worth it. Pine forests, snow in winter, green meadows in summer. Base for the Tungnath and Chandrashila trek — manageable even for beginners.
